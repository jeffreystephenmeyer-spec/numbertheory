Definition 3.1.1. Prime Number.

A prime number is a positive integer greater than 1 that has no positive integer divisors other than 1 and itself. In symbols, a positive integer \(p\) is prime if \(p>1\) and for all positive integers \(d\text{,}\) if \(d \mid p\text{,}\) then \(d=1\) or \(d=p\text{.}\)
