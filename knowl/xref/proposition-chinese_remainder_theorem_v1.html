Proposition 4.4.2. Chinese Remainder Theorem (Simple Version).

If \(m_1\) and \(m_2\) are relatively prime positive integers and \(a_1, a_2\) are integers, then the system of congruences \(\begin{cases} x \equiv a_1 \pmod{m_1} \\ x \equiv a_2 \pmod{m_2} \end{cases}\) has a unique solution modulo \(M = m_1 m_2\text{.}\)

Hint.

Try a direct proof. First show existence, then uniqueness. There exists integers \(s_1, s_2\)such that \(m_1s_1 + m_2s_2 = a_1-a_2\) (why???). Show \(x = a_1 - m_2s_2\) is a solution to the system of congruences. Now show that there is a unique solution modulo \(M\text{.}\) Suppose that \(x_1\) and \(x_2\) are two solutions. Then \(x_1 \equiv x_2 \pmod{m_1}\) and \(x_1 \equiv x_2 \pmod{m_2}\text{.}\) Show that this implies \(x_1 \equiv x_2 \pmod{mn}\text{.}\) You may find Definition 2.3.4, Proposition 2.3.2, Definition 2.4.1, Proposition 3.3.4, and Proposition 2.4.5 useful.
