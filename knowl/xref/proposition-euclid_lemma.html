Proposition 3.2.1. Euclid’s Lemma.

If \(p\in \Z\) is prime and \(p \mid bc\) for some \(b,c \in \Z\text{,}\) then \(p \mid b\) or \(p \mid c\text{.}\)
🔗
🔗

Hint.

Try a direct proof. Suppose \(p \nmid b\text{.}\) The \(\gcd(p,b)=1\) (why???). Deduce that there exist integers \(x,y\in \Z\) such that \(px+by=1\text{.}\) Use this to show that \(p \mid c\text{.}\) You may find Definition 3.1.1, Definition 2.2.2, Definition 2.1.1, Proposition 2.2.5 and Proposition 2.1.5 useful.
