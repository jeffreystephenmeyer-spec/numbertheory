Proposition 2.2.3. GCD and the Division Algorithm.

If \(a,b,q,r\in \Z\) where \(b > 0\) and \(a = bq+r \text{,}\) then \(\gcd(a,b) = \gcd(b,r)\text{.}\)

Hint.

Try a direct proof. First show that any common divisor of \(a\) and \(b\) is also a common divisor of \(b\) and \(r\text{.}\) Then show the converse. You may find Definition 2.1.1, Definition 2.2.1, Definition 2.2.2, Proposition 2.1.6 useful.
