Proposition 1.3.4. .

If \(R\) is a ordered ring and \(a,b,c\in R\) are positive such that \(a\) is (multiplicatively) invertible and \(0<b<c\text{,}\) then \(0<ab<ac\text{.}\)

🔗

Hint.

Try a proof by contradiction. We know it must be the case that \(0 \le ab \le ac\) (why???) but what could go wrong and how to we show those things cannot happen? Suppose one of the undesirable things happened and how does the invertibility of \(a\) lead to a contradiction? You may find Definition 1.2.1, Definition 1.3.2, Definition 1.3.5 useful.
