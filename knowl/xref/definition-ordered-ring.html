Definition 1.3.2. Ordered Ring.

A ring \(R\) is an ordered ring if \(R\) has a (total) order that is compatible with its operations by satisfying:
(OR1)
🔗
(translation.) If \(a,b,c \in R\) and \(a\le b\text{,}\) then \(a +c\le b+c\)
🔗
(OR2)
🔗
(scaling.) If \(a,b \in R\) satisfy \(0 \le a\) and \(0 \le b\text{,}\) then \(0\le ab\)
🔗
