Proposition 3.1.3. Product of integers greater than 1 increase size.

If \(a,b\in \Z\) are integers greater than 1, then \(0 < a < ab\text{.}\)
🔗
🔗

Hint.

Show \(0 \le b \le b+b=2b\text{.}\) Then show \(0 \le 2b \le 3b\text{.}\) Iterate. Recall that \(a = 1+1+\cdots + 1\) \(a\)-many times. Why can you rule out equality and get strict inequality? You may find Definition 1.2.2, and Proposition 1.3.7 useful.
