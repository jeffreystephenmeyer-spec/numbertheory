Definition 2.2.2. Greatest Common Divisor (GCD).

If \(a \) and \(b \) are integers, if \(g \) is a common divisor of \(a \) and \(b \) such that if \(d \) is any other common divisor of \(a \) and \(b \text{,}\) then \(g \ge d\text{,}\) then \(g \) is the greatest common divisor (gcd) of \(a \) and \(b \text{.}\)
