Proposition 5.1.3. Euler’s Criterion.

If \(p\) is an odd prime and \(\gcd(a,p)=1\text{,}\) then \(a\) is a quadratic residue modulo \(p\) if and only if \(a^{(p-1)/2} \equiv 1 \pmod{p}\text{.}\)

Hint.

First show the forward direction. Try a direct proof. Suppose \(a\) is a quadratic residue modulo \(p\text{.}\) Then there exists an integer \(x\) such that \(x^2 \equiv a \pmod{p}\text{.}\) Raise both sides to the power of \((p-1)/2\) and interpret. Now show the backward direction. Try a proof by contrapositive. Suppose \(a\) is not a quadratic residue modulo \(p\text{.}\) For each nonzero least residue mod \(p\text{,}\) \(r\text{,}\) there exists a unique and distinct least residue \(r'\) such that \(rr' \equiv a \pmod{p}\) (why???).
